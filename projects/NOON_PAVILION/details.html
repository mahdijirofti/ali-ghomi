Noon Pavilion
Noon Pavilion is a discrete 3d printed shell designed by Studio Musab. When I got the call from Musab Badahdah about this project, I was instantly very excited about it and I knew it would be a very fun project. They had a concept and a manufacturer who could print all of the pieces and assemble them in a reasonable time. All they needed was a fully detailed parametric model to bridge the gap between the renderings and fabrication. At the same time the conversation between the design team and fabrication team was still going and nothing was finalized. Including the module, dimensions of the shell, assembly process and the structure.
I built a parametric model that could adapt to all the changes, including but not limited to the shape and dimensions of the shell and the module. The definition also adjusts the outcome to the 3d printing constraints like overhang and flat cap, tags the modules based on their positions in the shell for the assembly, adds the details for the placements of the structural elements, and arrays the modules to be ready to print. We had a very tight deadline and I had to have the parametric model fully ready and functional in a couple of days so we could have some time to explore variations for the module and structural elements. One day towards the end of this process I got a panicked call that we needed to change the size of the structural elements and prepare all the modules from scratch. And it was done in a matter of seconds thanks to the power of parametric modeling. I was so happy to see something that could jeopardize the whole project was not an issue at all because we were prepared for it. There was a lot to learn in this project and I really appreciate the opportunity that was brought to me by Studio Musab.
