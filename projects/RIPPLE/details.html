The Ripple Collection
Light is one of the most mysterious things for me. We depend on it to understand our surroundings but it often tricks us and shows us amazing things that don’t exist. The sparkles of shiny sands on the beach or the colorful beam lights coming through a cathedral window. Although those sands are not gem stones and those colorful light beams are nothing but dust in the air, they are so beautiful that people travel long distances to experience them. The Ripple project is inspired by one of these gems. The effects of the reflected light from floating oil-based fluids over water. In the Ripple project I tried to isolate and reconfigure the patterns and colors of those reflections in three dimensions to create a unique and elegant aesthetics. I’m using this aesthetics to design series of home accessories, furnitures and environments.
Since the pattern is intricate and complex the simple form of the object creates a contrast that lets the eye to rest and circulate freely from one point to another. I used a complementary color pallet inspired by the Thin-film interference phenomenon. So when the pieces come together in harmony.
